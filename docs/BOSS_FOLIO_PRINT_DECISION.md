# Boss Folio Print Decision

## Problem

Legendary monsters, spellcasters, and higher-level bosses need more space than a normal front/back card. Shrinking text makes the card unusable in combat.

## Decision

Use two home-printer-friendly formats:

1. **Standard Fold-Over Card** for simple monsters.
   - Front and back are printed side by side.
   - User cuts one rectangle, folds on the spine, and laminates.

2. **Boss Folio / Accordion Card** for complex monsters.
   - Three card panels wide.
   - Six total faces of information.
   - Printed on standard letter cardstock.
   - User cuts, accordion-folds on spine marks, and laminates.

## Why

This keeps the printed product realistic for normal home printers while preserving complete combat information.

## Current Examples

- Goblin: standard fold-over card.
- Adult Black Dragon: Boss Folio.
- Lich: Boss Folio.

## Future Investigation

If Boss Folio becomes too bulky when laminated, test an alternate **Boss Decklet** format:

- Card 1: Art / Combat Summary
- Card 2: Actions / Traits
- Card 3: Spells / Legendary / Lair

The decklet would fit in a sleeve or binder pocket while avoiding awkward folds.
