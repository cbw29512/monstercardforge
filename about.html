About DM Forge

DM Forge is an independent, browser-based toolkit for preparing, running, printing, searching, and protecting tabletop campaigns. It is maintained through the public cbw29512/monstercardforge GitHub repository.

The product rule

Fast enough for the table. Clear enough to trust. Portable without a required account.

Every tool is designed to reduce pauses during play, produce readable player or DM materials, and keep private campaign details under the Dungeon Master’s control.

Rules methodology

Edition-sensitive formulas, lists, encounter tables, and published monster records are checked against current primary sources from Wizards of the Coast or D&D Beyond. Licensed stat-block data identifies its SRD version and license.

VerifiedPrimary source, date, declared scope, and regression test.
HeuristicOfficial data plus a clearly labeled DM Forge usability judgment.
HomebrewOriginal, campaign, or user-created behavior—not represented as official.

Internal saved values remain 2014 and 2024 for compatibility. Public labels use 5e (2014) and 5.5e (2024).

Privacy model

Detailed campaign data is stored in the current browser unless the DM deliberately exports or transfers it.

Shared inside DM Forge

Campaign names and privacy-safe record summaries used for navigation and counts.

Kept inside each tool

Session prep, full logs, NPC motives and secrets, loot details, item curses, artwork, encounter tactics, enemy HP, and complete combat records.

Player displays

Read-only allowlists send only public initiative information. They exclude enemy HP, AC, Dexterity, logs, prep, and DM notes.

Design and accessibility

The toolkit keeps a restrained fantasy identity while using modern application conventions:

  • Cinzel for display headings
  • System UI fonts for controls, forms, tables, and long sessions
  • Editorial serif typography for printed handouts
  • Contrast-tested colors and visible keyboard focus
  • Large touch controls and mobile-safe layouts
  • Reduced-motion support
  • Overflow detection and continuation pages for print

The active target is WCAG 2.2 AA. Physical device, screen-reader, and printer validation remains an ongoing release track.

Testing and change control

Automated tests protect:

  • JavaScript syntax and local asset links
  • Rules tables, spell differences, encounter edge cases, and monster data
  • Player/DM privacy boundaries
  • Host-only multiplayer authority
  • Print overflow safeguards
  • SEO metadata, sitemap coverage, color contrast, and typography contracts
  • Backup validation and cross-tool handoffs

Architectural decisions, rules sources, design tokens, rollback steps, and known limitations are recorded in the repository rather than relying on memory or chat history.

Primary sources and licensing

  • Current official D&D rules pages and changelog entries from D&D Beyond / Wizards of the Coast
  • SRD 5.1 and SRD 5.2.1 where open-license content is published under CC-BY-4.0
  • Official Google Search Central, web.dev, and W3C guidance for discovery, performance, and accessibility standards

DM Forge is independent and is not an official Wizards of the Coast product. D&D rules content, trademarks, and licensed material remain subject to their applicable ownership and license terms.

Known boundaries

  • The verified monster catalog currently contains a small 5e SRD sample set; expansion must remain source-traceable.
  • Optional lair and regional effects are excluded from current boss samples until separately sourced and tested.
  • Homebrew builders cannot guarantee that user-entered rules are balanced or rules-accurate.
  • Local browser storage requires regular backup; the Backup & Storage Center exists for that reason.
  • Multiplayer tools require internet signaling even when the rest of the local-first toolkit later gains offline support.
