# DM Forge Community Code of Conduct

DM Forge welcomes Dungeon Masters, players, designers, developers, accessibility testers, rules researchers, printers, and curious newcomers.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, patient, specific, and constructive.
- Critique ideas, designs, rules summaries, and code—not people.
- Assume good intent while still correcting inaccurate claims clearly.
- Share enough context to make feedback useful: tool, browser, ruleset, workflow, and expected result.
- Credit sources, creators, and contributors.
- Protect player privacy and campaign secrets.
- Keep official rules, licensed SRD material, DM Forge originals, and homebrew clearly distinguished.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized conduct, or personal attacks.
- Publishing another person’s private information.
- Spam, scams, impersonation, or deliberate misinformation.
- Posting pirated books, copied proprietary rules text, or other material that is not licensed for redistribution.
- Repeatedly derailing discussions after moderators ask for a change in behavior.
- Attempting to expose private DM records, player data, room information, or security-sensitive details.

## Moderation

Project maintainers and moderators may edit titles, move conversations, request clarification, lock threads, hide content, close requests, or remove contributions that violate these standards or create legal, privacy, security, or community risk.

Good-faith disagreements about product direction are welcome. A request may be declined because it conflicts with the project’s scope, privacy model, rules-verification requirements, licensing obligations, accessibility goals, or maintenance capacity.

## Reporting a problem

Use the repository’s private security-reporting path for security vulnerabilities when available. For community-conduct concerns, contact the maintainer through the GitHub account `cbw29512` rather than escalating the dispute publicly.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to DM Forge repositories, issues, discussions, pull requests, reviews, and project-controlled community spaces.
